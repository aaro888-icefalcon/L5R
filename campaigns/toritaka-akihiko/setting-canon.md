## Places
- **Toritaka Province** — the Falcon Minor Clan's home, a modest, mist-veiled land on the marches near the **Shinomen Forest**, the most haunted region in the Empire. The Falcon ward it against ghosts and yūrei. The Scorpion covet these lands.
- **Kyūden Toritaka** *(the Falcon's seat)* — Lord Hatsue's castle; quiet, austere, full of warding-charms.
- **Mukade no Tani — the Valley of the Centipede** — home of the Moshi; site of the **Shrine of the Lady Sun** and the **Light of the Lady Sun Dōjō**, where Akihiko trained. Reached by Mantis-built cliff-pulleys; the **Port of Sun's Reflection** trades to the sea.
- **The Shinomen Forest (edge)** — vast, ancient, haunted; kitsune, yūrei, and older things. The Falcon's eternal beat. Something old, hungry, and *learning* stirs in its deep heart (north).
- **The old hills (north & west)** — windswept, sparsely held Falcon highlands where the clan was young. Somewhere here lies the **reputed tomb of the First Yotogi**, the founder — and, by Hatsue's visions, the lost **First Ward**. *(Details to be discovered.)*
- **Akihiko's family keep (eastern border)** — a small keep his house holds on the Falcon's **eastern border**, where Toritaka, **Hare**, and **Crab** lands meet. His family is a **vassal house sworn to Lady Chiyo's line**; Akihiko is a border samurai & yōjimbō, and his usual duty is leading his squad on that tri-border march. The post he asked leave to return to (Sc.12) — and was refused, bound to the Champion instead.

## Relics / quests
- **The First Ward = *Asahi no Kagami,* the Dawn Mirror** — a bronze disc rimmed in jade, blessed by Amaterasu at the Empire's dawn; lies in the First Yotogi's barrow. **It is no weapon.** With it the First Yotogi *bound* (not killed) the deep power in chains of captured sunlight. **Waking it:** answers only to Lady Sun's true fire (Akihiko's Sacred sun-fire) kindled at it under an **open dawn**, and only to a willing soul ready to take up the binding **as a lasting duty** — to become the chain's new anchor (as the keeper / the First One were). *(Revealed by the Keeper, Scene 5.)* **THE PRICE (revealed by Hatsue, Scene 11):** the Mirror does not hold the dark on its own — its living **anchor** must *spend themselves* keeping the chain taut, consumed by slow degrees (self, soul, sight), as the First Yotogi, the Keeper (1200 years), and Hatsue herself (two years of her sight) were. To wake and wield it is to be devoured for the binding. Hatsue meant **Akihiko** to be that anchor — the price she could not bring herself to speak at the barrow. **Now (Sc.12):** the Mirror is no longer carried — Akihiko entrusted it to **Toritaka Yoshi** & the Yotogi priests for safekeeping & study while he searches, on Lady Chiyo's charge, for a way to wield it that does *not* consume its anchor (a second anchor, a loophole in the founder's lore, or another road entirely).
- **The deep power (revealed by the Keeper):** older than the Empire, older than the Kami; a nameless thing of the deep cold earth that **cannot die and was never alive.** Its one fear is **the sun.** It can only be **bound**, never slain — trying to kill it *feeds* it. It has gnawed the First Yotogi's binding thin for 1200 years; now it harvests souls to pull the chains loose from within.
- **Akihiko's Mark = danger AND key (revealed by the Keeper):** the power's hook in him is mortal peril (it is how it took the Keeper) — *and* a tether: "to bind a thing you must first have hold of it." The First Yotogi bore such a mark *willingly* to chain the dark. Akihiko's wound may be the **leash** by which he drags the power to the Mirror's face.

## Named NPCs (continued)
- **The Keeper of the Barrow** — the bound grave-fire spirit of an ancient Falcon (a watchman set by the First Yotogi), who guarded the Dawn Mirror for 1200 years. Lucid, noble, dying — the deep power's rot ("grey") is consuming it, and when it guts out it will **turn** into the dark's creature. **Bargain struck (Sc.5):** it taught Akihiko all it knew of the Mirror, the enemy, and the mark; in return Akihiko swore to **end it cleanly with Lady Sun's fire before the rot turns it,** and to take up the Dawn Mirror and its duty. *(The mercy-kill collides head-on with his Softheartedness.)*

## Factions & clans in play (name — agenda — disposition to the PC)
- **Falcon Clan (Toritaka)** — ward the marches against the spirit-dark; survive as a dismissed Minor Clan — *Akihiko's clan; home.*
- **Centipede Clan (Moshi)** — sing the sun awake, heal, guard Amaterasu's shrine — *his father's people; warm toward him; his second home.*
- **Mantis Clan (Yoritomo)** — the Centipede's oldest allies; sea-trade and rough vigor — *friendly.*
- **Scorpion Clan** — seize Toritaka Province by scheme, blackmail, and patience — **hostile; they bar their doors to him (Scorn).**
- **Phoenix Clan** — the Empire's self-appointed voice of the kami; regard Moshi sun-worship as crude folk-faith — *disdainful (religious snobbery).*
- **Lion Clan (Akodo/Matsu/Ikoma)** — martial paragons of Bushidō; offering (or being *used* to offer) a **protectorate** that would absorb the Falcon as a vassal. Direct, not subtle — "a Lion who wanted your province would ride up the valley with three thousand spears and a poem about it." Their hand in the surrender may be genuine ambition, or another's craft wearing a Lion face. *(Matsu Toshimoko, Emerald Magistrate, is Lion.)*
- **Crab Clan (Hida/Hiruma/Yasuki)** — the Falcon's **ancient allies**; wall-builders against the dark, kin in spirit to a clan that wards ghosts. Border the Falcon to the south/east — the natural ally the Lion deal conspicuously bypasses. *(Genzō & Bara of the Scattered Corps are Crab.)*
- **Unicorn Clan** — hold lands to the **Falcon's north**; another power whose protection was *not* sought — a question Akihiko raised and Chiyo has not answered.
- **Hare Clan** — a fellow Minor Clan; Akihiko's usual border post stands at the **Hare–Falcon–Crab tri-border**.

## Named NPCs (name — role — want — secret)
- **Toritaka Hatsue, "the Yotogi"** — Falcon Clan Champion / daimyō; spirit-seer who has ruled ~12 years; banished hauntings in the Shinomen and once in a Lion castle. Want: hold the line against the spirit-dark. **Secret (revealed to Akihiko):** her "fraying mind" is no madness — for two years she has been *spending* her sight staring into the deep Shinomen at the waking power, which is why she withdraws. She has charged Akihiko **in secret** to recover the First Ward, named his not-belonging as why she trusts him, and hinted she senses "more to you than your modesty admits" (re: his hidden blood?). *(Akihiko's lord & hard mentor; secret-sharer; the one who chose him.)* **DIED at her deathbed (Scene 11)** — she received the Mirror, named Akihiko *"the other inheritance"* (the clan's ward, not the seat), revealed the Mirror's price, and passed (→ `archive.md`).
- **Toritaka Chiyo** — Hatsue's eldest daughter; iron-willed, the scariest of Akihiko's peers (broke a Lion's nose at the winter tournament without changing expression). **Lady of the Falcon / Clan Champion (Sc.11).** **Made Akihiko her *yoriki* (Sc.12)** — *hers, not the clan's* — with a three-part charge (the Mirror's price; the forger of the Lion-lie; watching her own blind side). Refused to be rushed into the Lion deal; suspects it was *engineered* and set Akihiko to find the hand. Named the 'cousin'/vassal distinction between their houses (his family is sworn to hers) as **deliberate elevated trust**, not blood. **Secret (Sc.12 interview):** beneath the iron she hides a specific FEAR — walled off hard the instant Akihiko's 'why me' brushed it (oracle: Exceptional No), and *not* the Lion-lie (she shields no personal suspect there). She charged him to name the things she's 'too proud or too frightened to see' — licensing him to hunt the very thing she conceals. *(Akihiko's peer-become-liege.)*
- **Toritaka Ujiyasu** — the clan's aged *karō* (seneschal), who has run the Toritaka household for generations. A frightened pragmatist, not a villain: facing a drowning Minor Clan and a heirless death, he brokered a **Lion protectorate** (vassalage for survival) that Hatsue refused. Now presses Lady Chiyo for the seal.
- **Toritaka Akiro** — Akihiko's younger brother; apprentice healer-shugenja; calm, kind, devoted. Want: become a true healer and stay at his brother's side. *(Akhil — the dearest bond.)*
- **Moshi Hizashi** — Akihiko's father; brilliant, scattered, entrepreneurial Centipede sun-priest-physician; a "radical" who treats invocations as natural philosophy (after the Agasha). Want: prove his heretical healing-methods. *(Bhaskar.)*
- **Toritaka Sumiko** — Akihiko's mother; retired, grounded Falcon healer-warden; the family bedrock. Daughter of **Seppun Yuki** — through her runs the family's **Seppun (Imperial) blood**, openly acknowledged within the house, not a buried secret. *(Sudha.)*
- **Suzu** — reed-cutter's daughter of Hibari Mura, ~16; once sang the dawn-hymn Akihiko taught. **Sole survivor** of the soul-harvest; he saved her soul at the threshold. Now stable but deeply soul-shocked, sleeping; bonded to her rescuer, and the only living witness to the village's fate. *(Q16-adjacent / new bond.)*
- **Toritaka Yuki** — Akihiko's grandmother (mother's side); **born Seppun**, a minor scion of the Imperial guardian-family that stands between the Throne and the spirit-dark, before she married into the Toritaka and made herself the household's matriarch — its terror and its law. Scary as a drawn blade; a real soft spot for Akihiko. She is the source of the family's **known Imperial thread** (Seppun blood), which is why a Falcon backwater house sits a notch above its station. Keeper of the old family stories. *(grandmother; Bergen-County kin.)*

- **Gennai** — a Scorpion spymaster who posed as a charming traveling medicine-peddler to intercept Akihiko on the only road into the old hills. Sharp, affable, a reader of people; traveled with a disguised swordsman ("guard") and a covert courier ("porter"). Saw through Akihiko's cover and got a message out about the Falcon's secret errand. **Run down and CAPTURED (Sc.9)** — sword-hand maimed, handed to the Emerald Magistrate in irons; held in Kyūden Toritaka's cellar as the one live thread to the Scorpion craft behind the Lion-lie. **SILENCED in his cell overnight after Sc.12** by an inside Scorpion hand before he could be interrogated — proof the enemy already walks the Falcon's halls, and that what he knew was worth a murder inside a Champion's castle. *(The lead cut; now a murder to solve.)*
- **The captured porter** — Gennai's courier, a low Scorpion operative taken alive on the hill; sullen, dangerous, and holds what he knows about handlers, the message, and the cell.
- **Matsu Toshimoko** — **Emerald Magistrate** (Lion/Matsu), lean and gray-templed, by-the-book, used to obedience. Riding the marches eleven days over the silenced villages and Scorpion border-testing when her patrol met Akihiko's at a night ford (Sc.8) and watched a soul-harvest puppet attack. Witnessed him free the boy with Lady Sun's fire; respects the deed, distrusts the secrecy and the broken composure. **Deferred, not won** — rides to Kyūden Toritaka for Lord Hatsue's answers, Imperial authority in reserve. Does not yet know of the Mirror or the Seppun blood. *(recurring Imperial figure; an Emerald Magistrate ↔ a secretly Imperial-blooded Falcon, neither fully knowing.)*
- **The freed boy of Hibari Mura** — a reed-cutter, soul-harvested with his village, puppeted across the ford by the deep power and freed when Akihiko seared the thread with Sacred fire (Sc.8). Body alive and reclaimed; soul still gathered in the deep — soul-shocked and empty, a second living survivor like Suzu, and living proof the taken can be partly reclaimed.

- **Ikoma Naoki** — a Lion (Ikoma) courtier-diplomat; the velvet face who carried the **Lion protectorate** offer to karō Ujiyasu three times, the brushwork too polished for a thing dreamt up in a panic. Named by Chiyo (Sc.12) as the Lion side of the engineered surrender — a genuine Lion grasping a real opening, or a mouth with another hand up the back of it? The first living thread of the Lion-lie now that Gennai is dead. *(Not yet met.)*
- **Toritaka Yoshi** — head of the Falcon's **Yotogi priests** (the clan's spirit-warding priesthood). Akihiko handed him the Dawn Mirror for safekeeping & study (Sc.12); keeper of the binding-lore, and the natural ally in the search for a way to wield the Mirror without being consumed by it. *(New NPC, Sc.12.)*

## Timeline / recent events
- Akihiko, Falcon-born of a Moshi father, completed his training as a Moshi Sun Sentinel and returned to serve Lord Hatsue as Warden of the Physical.
- The Scorpion have begun quiet maneuvers toward Toritaka lands; doors that were open to the Falcon now close.
- Lord Hatsue's meditations grow longer and stranger.
- **Hibari Mura, soul-emptied (Scene 1).** A gaki, *Kuchinashi the Mouthless*, made the village's sun-shrine a soul-gate and harvested its ~40 people. Akihiko & Jōji arrived too late; Akihiko sealed the gate with Sacred sun-fire, banishing the gaki but recovering only **Suzu** (the lone survivor). The rest were dragged down/north into the deep Shinomen — **taken, not destroyed.**
- **Revealed by commune (canon now):** *No mortal opened that gate.* The power in the deep Shinomen reached across the leagues on its own, strong enough to **corrupt a consecrated shrine of Amaterasu from afar and reach through it.** The Falcon's warding shrines may no longer be safe.
- **Lord Hatsue died; Chiyo succeeds (Scene 11–12).** The Falcon confirmed **Lady Chiyo** as Clan Champion at an oath-renewal; Akihiko — who recovered the Mirror and steered the succession — was named her **yoriki**. The Dawn Mirror passed to the **Yotogi priests (Yoshi)** for keeping.
- **The Lion-lie (canon now, Sc.12).** The "protectorate" rests on a **manufactured threat** — Scorpion raids that didn't quite happen, moved border-stones, a magistrate's patrol misread as a noose — stacked into the lie that *the Falcon can't hold its border and must shelter under the Lion.* The CRAFT reads Scorpion; the GAIN is the Lion's; the two don't sum. **Ikoma Naoki** is the Lion broker; **Ujiyasu** the unwitting dupe. Open question: **would the Throne even sanction** a Minor Clan being swallowed whole? Akihiko's private dread: a Falcon dissolved into the Lion is a Falcon that stops warding the Shinomen — which would *serve the deep power.*
- **Gennai silenced (overnight after Sc.12).** The captured Scorpion spymaster — the one live thread to the craft — was murdered in Kyūden Toritaka's cellar by an inside hand before he could be interrogated. The enemy is within the walls.
