## ▶ Scene-by-scene chronicle
**Scene 1 — The Silent Hamlet (Hibari Mura).** On patrol, Akihiko & Jōji found a Falcon border hamlet soul-emptied: a gaki, **Kuchinashi the Mouthless**, had fouled the village's sun-shrine into a soul-gate and harvested its ~40 people. A skirmish: steel was useless (Insubstantial); Akihiko's Sacred sun-fire was the key. He **sealed the gate** with a climactic Theology check — banishing Kuchinashi for good and saving the lone survivor, **Suzu** — but ~39 souls were dragged into the deep Shinomen (not destroyed). The strain **unmasked** him (the Falcon mask shattered; he sang the dawn-hymn in grief).

**Scene 2 — The Yotogi's Dawn.** He carried Suzu home to **Kyūden Toritaka** and reported, in full, to a lucid, formidable **Lord Hatsue**. She revealed her "madness" is no madness — she has *seen* the deep power for two years — and charged him, **in secret** ("three mouths: her, you, your soldier"), to recover **the First Ward**, the First Yotogi's lost sealing-relic, from the founder's tomb (north & west). He demurred (unworthy); she refused his modesty and named his not-belonging as why she trusts him. He accepted, left Suzu with **Akiro**, and rode out with **Jōji + a troop of ashigaru**.

**Scene 3 — A Meeting on the Road.** A smooth "peddler," **Gennai**, joined them and probed for their destination; Akihiko's thin cover failed. He rode on and watched — and at night caught Gennai's "porter" slipping off to a hooded contact. He roused the camp and **charged**, but his horse stumbled on shale: **the message escaped (the leak succeeded — limited: that a Falcon Warden rides the hills secretly).** He captured the porter; **Gennai and his swordsman fled.** He left the porter bound "for the Kami," kept doubled watch, and the **dawn-rite cleared his strife.**

**Scene 4 — The Reaching Cold.** In a misty defile, the deep power tore a **portal** and reached for Akihiko *personally.* He met it with Sacred fire + a Void point — but the ward fell **one short**; the touch landed: **Marked by the Deep.** His sun-fire seared it back, and a **vision** came: the stolen souls *survive*, gathered deep; the power is ancient and sun-fearing; the **First Ward truly bound it once.** He steadied the men with dry calm, **confided everything to Jōji**, then rested, communed, and worked a **Cleansing Rite that contained the mark** (beacon blunted).

**Scene 5 — The Broken Wards / The Dawn Mirror.** They reached the founder's tomb to find its ancient wards **shattered**. The **Keeper** (a dying, lucid grave-fire spirit) chose to **bargain**, not fight. Akihiko accepted — but first, respectfully, asked to be **taught**, elder to child. Moved, the Keeper spent its last days teaching him (the lore below), then named the price: **end it cleanly with sun-fire before the rot turned it.** Against his **Softheartedness**, Akihiko **kept the bargain** — the mercy-stroke; the Keeper (**Toritaka Genkō**) passed whole to Yomi, naming itself as a parting gift. Akihiko entered the barrow and lifted **Asahi no Kagami**; it **woke to his blood, confirming his Imperial lineage**, and he **took up the binding-duty.**

## ▶ Key canon / lore established
- **The First Ward = Asahi no Kagami, the Dawn Mirror** — bronze, jade-rimmed, blessed by Amaterasu. **Binds (never kills)** the deep power in captured sunlight. **Wakes** to Akihiko's Sacred sun-fire at an **open dawn**, by a willing soul who takes the binding **as a lasting duty** (the chain's new anchor — which Akihiko now is). Held by Akihiko.
- **The deep power** — unkillable, only **bound**; fears only the sun; harvests souls; bound once by the First Yotogi 1200 years ago; gnawing free ever since.
- **The Mark (Marked by the Deep)** — danger AND **key**: it is the **leash** by which Akihiko can drag the power to the Mirror. **Contained** (beacon blunted) but only the Mirror (or the power's undoing) truly cures it.
- **Imperial blood — CONFIRMED.** The Mirror knew Akihiko as kin to Amaterasu's line. *How* a Falcon Minor-Clan warden carries the Emperor's blood is the live mystery (and a thing others would kill or use him for).
