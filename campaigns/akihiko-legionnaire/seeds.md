# Seed Deck — Akihiko, Chūi of the Imperial Legions

> The live deck of **30–40 candidate beats** the GM draws on for scenes, Turning Points, and Random
> Events. **Refreshed at every bookkeeping.** A seed is a *candidate*, not a script — the dice still
> decide if and how one fires. Sources: canon near the PC · live world state · bridge generators · L5R hooks.

## Deck (size target 30–40)
**The front & the war**
- An order comes down to spend Akihiko's cohort on a feint that buys the main force time — his ninjō vs his giri, hour one.
- A Lion officer demands the Legion stand aside "for one hour" so honor can be answered at Toshi Ranbo; refusing risks a fight, allowing it breaks the edict.
- A Crane envoy offers Akihiko a gift/poem — courtesy that is also a hook, testing whether the Throne's referee can be turned.
- A skirmish erupts at a contested ford/wall when neither army will yield the road; Akihiko's company is between them.
- The edict's exact wording is ambiguous; both clans read it their way and look to the junior Imperial officer to rule.
- A captured Lion (or Crane) soldier will be executed by the other side unless Akihiko intervenes — Softheartedness fires.
- A mass-battle flashpoint: the two armies commit; the Legion must decide whether to enforce by blood.

**His cohort (the people he's sworn to keep alive)**
- An ashigaru in his squad is a frightened farmer three years into a six-year term; his death would be "acceptable losses."
- A veteran sergeant under him quietly tests the new chūi: is the priest-nephew soft, or worth following?
- Supplies are short; the quartermaster's slate values rice over the wounded — a Medicine/Command crossroads.
- One of his soldiers commits a dishonorable act (looting, desertion, a killing); Akihiko must judge — punish, shield, or report.

**Command & the patron's leash**
- A letter from uncle Seppun Tadanobu: praise wrapped around an instruction Akihiko won't like.
- His commanding officer (a taisa — generate) gives him a task designed to test loyalty over conscience.
- A peer officer resents the black-sheep nephew's placement and works to see him fail or look weak.
- A superior asks Akihiko to report on a clan's conduct — making him the Throne's informer.
- Tadanobu's favor is "noticed" by someone who wants to use Akihiko to reach the uncle.

**Heritage & court**
- A high-Status courtier treats Akihiko's blood as currency — courting, or testing, or baiting him.
- Someone airs the grandfather's scandal to shame him in front of soldiers or peers.
- An Imperial (Otomo/Miya/Seppun) figure arrives at the front with their own agenda for the edict.
- A marriage or alliance is floated that would use his bloodline — a leash dressed as honor.

**The spirit-dark (the Falcon's eye)**
- His Sixth Sense pricks at a battlefield thick with the unquiet dead; something is feeding on the war.
- A "haunting" the soldiers whisper of is real — a yūrei or gaki drawn to the slaughter (the Falcon's true trade).
- A shugenja's invocation goes wrong, or a shrine on the line is defiled; the kami are watching the war.
- Akihiko's sun-fire is needed where steel is useless — and using it openly reveals what he is.

**Personal / anxiety triggers**
- A high-stakes check whose failure would harm his dependents (Fear of Failure fires).
- A kill-or-spare choice with no clean answer (Softheartedness fires; 1 Void first time/scene).
- The Scorpion bar a door he needs open (Scorn of the Scorpion).
- Word from home: his brother Akiro, or the haunted Toritaka lands, needs him while duty holds him at the front.

## Live world — after the toll-house (Sc.2 aftermath)
- **Taisa Seppun Saburō hears the news at dawn** — the post fell, the Lion crossed, the patron's pup lost a duel and lives by a Matsu's mercy. Cold contempt confirmed, or something unexpected — a charge, a punishment, a test redoubled?
- The two riders (Sora + one) reach the Legion camp; how they tell it shapes how it's heard — and whether the boy embellishes his lord's grace or his lord's defeat.
- Akihiko's Falcons saw their officer lose and be spared. Morale fork: shameful, or a lord who'd die for the edict and was honored by a great-clan blade? Yoshi's read sets the tone.
- **Matsu Kasumi's debt comes due** — a token arrives, or she crosses his path again (court, parley, the next clash), and "remember who gave it to you" turns from words into a claim.
- Word spreads through both armies: the Falcon priest who taunted a Matsu, fought to the death, and was spared. A courtier, a rival officer, or a Lion who disagrees with Kasumi's mercy takes an interest.
- The Lion who crossed west had a destination — what did Kasumi's patrol go west *for*? (The "lawful business" she never named.)
- A superior (or Tadanobu, by letter) demands a written accounting for the broken edict; the black-sheep nephew must explain a failure on paper.
- A Crane party noticed the breach (the horn carried west too); the Left Hand's people now know an Imperial post let the Lion through — and may use it.
- The ninjō/giri lesson lands cold: this time grace saved him; next time it's a battlefield, and grace doesn't stop arrows. When does the choice come for *real* — his Falcons' lives against an order?
