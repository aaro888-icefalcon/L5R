## Ninjō (desire) & Giri (duty) — the heart of the drama
*(Both pull toward the Falcon, in opposite directions — that is the tragedy: the honor he owes his clan is bought with the lives of his clan.)*
- **Ninjō — *Bring my soldiers home alive to the Falcon.*** A healer at heart given a sword and a command, Akihiko cannot see the men under him as the Empire sees them — renewable, expendable, a number on a quartermaster's slate. His cohort carries a **Falcon (Toritaka) contingent** serving the Legion under their famous-blooded son — clansmen, neighbors, the marches he grew up on. He wants every one of them to march home to the Valley of the Spirits alive. In the Imperial Legions — where peasants sign six-year terms and great battles are won by spending lives — this desire collides with duty at nearly every order.
- **Giri — *Win the Emperor's wars to bring honor to the Falcon.*** Sworn to the Emperor's own army: hold the line, enforce Imperial edicts between feuding clans, win the wars the Throne commands — *whatever the cost in lives his ninjō would spare.* A minor clan of little political weight rises only by deeds done in the Empire's sight; every victory under the Imperial banner is a notch of standing for Toritaka, a step out of the black-sheep shadow. His commission came through the sponsorship of his illustrious uncle, **Seppun Tadanobu**; he serves under officers who know exactly whose nephew — and whose black-sheep grandson — he is. *To honor the Falcon, he must spend the Falcon.*

## Heritage — the known Seppun blood
Akihiko's Imperial descent is **no secret**; it is openly known, and it is *complicated*.
- **Maternal grandfather — Toritaka Michio (born Seppun Michio):** a genuinely **talented** Seppun who became the **black sheep** of the Imperial family by **leaving the Seppun to marry a Toritaka** — trading the honor of guarding the Emperor's person for a haunted minor clan on the marches. The family never quite forgave the slight. *(deceased)*
- **Illustrious maternal uncle — Seppun Tadanobu:** Michio's son, Akihiko's mother's brother, who **distinguished himself** in Imperial service and was **reclaimed into the Seppun** — partly **redeeming** the line's standing and keeping the bloodline's name known. Akihiko's sponsor into the Legions; a powerful, watchful patron whose favor is also a leash.
- **Net:** openly-acknowledged Imperial blood, but from the *scandalous branch* — high enough that others court or resent him, low enough that he is always the black sheep's grandson with something to prove, riding on his uncle's prestige.
